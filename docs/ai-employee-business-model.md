# M&T Services — The "AI Employee" Business Model

> A plan for selling AI agents that fill a real job role inside a business,
> how to find companies that need them, and a ready-to-use pitch.

---

## 1. The Big Idea: Sell an Outcome, Not Software

Most agencies sell "a chatbot" or "a website." That's a **tool** — the buyer
has to figure out how to get value from it. Instead, sell an **AI Employee**: a
trained agent that does a specific job a business already pays a human (or wishes
it could afford to) to do.

Reframe everything around a single sentence the owner already understands:

> "We give you a tireless team member who works 24/7, never calls in sick, and
> costs less than a part-time hire."

You're not competing with other web designers anymore — you're competing with the
cost of hiring, and you win on price every time.

### Roles your AI agents can fill

| AI Employee | Replaces / Augments | What it does | Suggested price |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AI Receptionist** | Front desk / answering service | Answers calls, chats & FAQs, books appointments, routes urgent issues | $349–$649/mo |
| **AI Sales Rep (SDR)** | Lead-chaser / inside sales | Instant text-back, qualifies leads, follows up until they book | $499–$999/mo |
| **AI Scheduler / Dispatcher** | Office admin | Books, confirms, reschedules, sends reminders, fills cancellations | $299–$599/mo |
| **AI Support Agent** | Tier-1 customer support | Handles "where's my order," returns, how-to, escalates the rest | $399–$799/mo |
| **AI Marketing Assistant** | Part-time marketer | Blogs, social posts, Google Business updates, review replies | $299–$599/mo |
| **AI Reputation Manager** | Reviews coordinator | Requests reviews, replies to all reviews in brand voice | $199–$399/mo |

> **Pricing anchor:** A part-time hire at $15/hr × 20 hrs/wk ≈ **$1,300/mo** before
> taxes, mistakes, and turnover. Price your AI Employee at **15–40% of a human**
> and the ROI sells itself.

## 2. The Offer Structure

Keep it parallel to the website subscription tiers so it's easy to upsell:

1. **One AI Employee** — pick a single role (most clients start here).
2. **AI Department** — 2–3 agents that hand off to each other (e.g., Receptionist
   → Scheduler → Reputation Manager). Bundle at ~20% off the à-la-carte total.
3. **Done-for-you setup fee** (optional): a small one-time onboarding ($299–$999)
   to train the agent on their business, scripts, calendar, and tone. This covers
   your labor and filters tire-kickers, while the monthly subscription is the
   real engine.

**Why subscription wins here:** an AI Employee needs ongoing tuning, monitoring,
and content — so recurring revenue is honest, not a gimmick. Target **net revenue
retention > 100%**: land one agent, expand into a department over time.

### Unit economics (illustrative)

- Price: **$499/mo** for one AI Sales Rep.
- Your cost: AI/API + tooling + hosting ≈ **$40–$120/mo** per client.
- Gross margin: **~80%+**.
- 20 clients = **~$10k MRR** at very low delivery cost.
- The work is mostly **setup + monitoring**, which scales far better than custom builds.

## 3. How to Find Businesses That Need This (Lead Sourcing)

You're hunting for two signals: **(a) they're hiring for a role an agent can do**,
or **(b) they're visibly losing money to a gap an agent fills** (missed calls, no
website, dead reviews, slow replies).

### A. Mine job boards for "replaceable" roles
Search **Indeed, ZipRecruiter, LinkedIn Jobs, Craigslist gigs, Facebook Jobs**, and
local "now hiring" posts for titles like:

- "Receptionist," "Front Desk," "Appointment Setter," "Scheduler"
- "Customer Service Rep," "Call Center Agent," "Virtual Assistant"
- "Inside Sales," "Lead Coordinator," "Intake Specialist"

**The pitch angle:** *"I saw you're hiring a receptionist. Before you spend $30k+/yr,
would you look at an AI receptionist that does the repetitive 80% for a few hundred
a month — so your new hire (or you) only handles the human 20%?"*
You're not always replacing the hire; you're shrinking the workload and the cost.

### B. Spot the gaps (no job post needed)
Local businesses leaking money are everywhere. Build a target list by scanning
**Google Maps / Google Business Profiles** in your area (Bossier City, Shreveport,
then outward) for:

- **No website** or an outdated one → website + AI Receptionist combo.
- **Few/old reviews or unanswered reviews** → AI Reputation Manager.
- **"Closed" but you know demand is 24/7** (HVAC, plumbing, med spas, law, dental,
  auto, real estate) → after-hours AI Receptionist that captures the leads they
  currently miss.
- **Call it yourself after hours** — if no one answers, that's your opening line.

### C. Best-fit industries (high missed-call cost)
Home services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, lawn), dental & medical/med spas,
law firms, real estate & property management, auto repair/detailing, salons & barbers,
gyms & studios, restaurants doing catering/reservations. These bleed revenue on every
missed call — exactly what an AI Employee stops.

### D. A simple weekly prospecting routine
1. Pull **20 new job posts** for replaceable roles in your region.
2. Pull **20 businesses** from Google Maps with a visible gap.
3. Personalize one opening line per prospect (the gap you found).
4. Reach out via email, DM, and a quick Loom/video showing a demo agent answering
   *their* FAQ. Your own site's chat widget is already the live proof.
5. Follow up 3–4 times. Most sales are in the follow-up.

## 4. The Pitch

### 30-second version (cold call / DM / networking)
> "Hi — I build AI employees for local businesses. The most popular one is an AI
> receptionist that answers your calls and messages 24/7, books appointments, and
> texts back every lead in seconds — so you stop losing customers to voicemail.
> It runs about a tenth the cost of a part-time hire and it's live in days. Can I
> send you a 2-minute demo answering questions about *your* business?"

### Email / message template (use the gap you found)
> **Subject: the calls [Business Name] is missing after 5pm**
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I called [Business Name] last night at 7:42pm and got voicemail — totally normal,
> but every one of those is a customer who'll just call the next [plumber/dentist/shop].
>
> I build **AI receptionists** for businesses like yours. Yours would answer every
> call and website chat 24/7, book the appointment, and text the lead back instantly
> — in your voice, trained on your services. It's live in a few days and costs about
> **$349/mo**, far less than a part-time front desk.
>
> Want me to spin up a free demo that answers questions about [Business Name]
> specifically? Takes me 10 minutes and you'll see exactly how it sounds.
>
> — Myles, M&T Services · (270) 570-0790

### The value framing that closes (say it out loud)
1. **Cost of inaction:** "How many calls do you miss a week? If even two a month
   become customers at $[avg ticket], the agent pays for itself many times over."
2. **Vs. hiring:** "A part-time receptionist is $1,300+/mo, gets sick, and goes home
   at 5. This is a few hundred, works 24/7, and never quits."
3. **Risk reversal:** "Month-to-month, cancel anytime. Try it 30 days — if it doesn't
   book you appointments, walk away."
4. **Proof:** Show the live demo answering *their* questions. Seeing it beats
   explaining it.

### Handling the top objection — "Will it sound robotic / annoy my customers?"
> "Fair concern. It's trained on your business and your tone, it always offers a
> human handoff, and you get every transcript. Most customers just want a fast,
> correct answer at 9pm — which is exactly when your competitors send them to
> voicemail."

## 5. 30-Day Launch Plan

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| **1** | Productize one role (AI Receptionist). Build a repeatable setup checklist + demo template. Use your own site's chat widget as the live showcase. |
| **2** | Build a target list: 40 prospects (20 from job boards, 20 from Google Maps gaps). Draft the email/DM + a reusable demo video script. |
| **3** | Outreach: 10 personalized touches/day. Offer a free custom demo to every warm reply. Book consultations. |
| **4** | Close 1–3 founding clients at a "charter" rate, over-deliver, collect a testimonial + a measurable win (calls answered, leads booked). Use it as the proof for the next 40. |

**North-star metric:** booked demos → closed agents → expansion into departments.
Land the receptionist, then grow the account into a full AI team.
